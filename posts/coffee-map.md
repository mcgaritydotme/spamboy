Wherever I land, the first thing I build is a mental map of where to get a decent flat white. Here is the unscientific method I have refined over far too many cups.

## The rules

1. Never the place on the main square. Never.
2. Look for the shop with no menu in English and a queue of people who clearly walked there.
3. If they have a single-origin board and a tired expression, you&rsquo;re home.

## Why it matters

It sounds frivolous. It isn&rsquo;t, quite. A good café is the fastest way to feel like a temporary local rather than a tourist &mdash; somewhere to sit, watch, and let a city arrange itself around you for an hour.

That hour has rescued more travel days than any itinerary I&rsquo;ve ever written.
