There is a particular kind of thinking that only happens at 70mph in the dark, with a stranger asleep across the aisle and your phone refusing to find signal.

## The compartment

I&rsquo;d booked a couchette without quite understanding what one was. It turns out to be a shelf. A comfortable shelf, with a small reading light and a window blind that does nothing to stop the platform lights strobing past at every station.

I loved it immediately.

## What you notice

When you can&rsquo;t do anything, you start *seeing* things. The way the conductor balances against the sway without thinking. The exact shade of orange the sodium lamps throw across a sleeping valley. The fact that everyone, everywhere, lowers their voice on a night train.

> Slow travel isn&rsquo;t about the destination being far. It&rsquo;s about giving the journey permission to be the point.

I arrived in Vienna underslept and entirely happy. Recommended.
